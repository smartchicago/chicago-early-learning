Are you ready to apply for preschool for your 3 or 4 year old?

Enrolling your 3 or 4 year-old child in an early learning program is easy, and open year-round. Follow these easy steps to get your child enrolled in preschool:

Step 1) Click here to create an online account for your family

Step 2) Fill in information on your family, such as employment and income, information on your child, and then apply for your top choice preschool site

Step 3) If your child qualifies, you will receive an email on June 1st with information on how to verify your child’s preschool seat

See you for the first day of school in September!

If you have questions, please call our Chicago Early Learning Hotline: (312) 229-1690.

Have you found the right program for your child?

The City of Chicago and its partners offer early learning programs at over 600 sites throughout the city. Visit our search page to identify sites in areas near your home or work. The Chicago Early Learning Portal allows you to identify a program’s quality rating, schedule and ages served.

We encourage you to reach out to preferred providers to ask questions or schedule a visit. Resources to help identify if a program is a good fit for your family are included on our family information page.

Need application support?

If you have any concerns with the online application, please call our Family Support Hotline at (312) 229-1690, or visit any of the Family Resource Centers listed below.

You are welcome to visit a Chicago Public School or community-based preschool site directly, within their operating hours, for additional support.

How will you verify your child's placement?

If your child is offered a preliminary preschool placement, you will receive an email with verification instructions.

*List of Documents that are generally accepted for verification:

Proof of child’s age

  • Original birth certificate
  • Passport

Proof of address

  • Driver’s license/ID card
  • Utility bill
  • Lease agreement/mortgage statement
  • Current voter’s registration card

Proof of income

  • Current paystub
  • 1040/W-2
  • SSI Letter
  • Unemployment letter/stub
  • Workers’ compensation letter/stub
  • Retirement or pension stub/letter
  • Alimony
  • Official child support letter
  • TANF
  • Income verification form

Note: If your family is in a temporary living situation, you are not required to present the documents listed above for verification. All schools must immediately enroll students in temporary living situations even if the student lacks health, immunization or school records, proof of guardianship, proof of residency, or any other documentation normally required for school enrollment.
